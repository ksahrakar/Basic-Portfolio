About me

Learning coding at 55 is not normal. Nor is learning coding in retirement. Nor is retiring from brain surgery at 55. I am delighted to be a three-time abnormal!

Having my first career behind me, I am in the position to pursue my other interests and to realize some of the ideas I have accumulated in frustration during my first career. During my practice not a day would pass that I would not encounter an inefficency in programming or suboptimal interaction with technology that could be easily remedied with good prgramming and user interfaces. Frustrations my colleagues and my profession were experiencing in progressing to a more technologically savvy world could be remedied with better programming. I hope to be able to apply my new skills to becoming a conduit between the fast-moving information technology advancements and the health care provider.
